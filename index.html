European Labour Market Analysis

A full end-to-end SQL analysis of employment and unemployment trends across 35 European countries from 2015 to 2024. // 02 — insights

Key Findings

41pp

Gender employment gap in Turkey (prime age 25-54) — the largest in Europe, more than 8x Austria's gap of 4pp

-5.8pp

Montenegro's employment drop during COVID-19 in 2020 — the steepest decline across all 35 countries

4×

Youth unemployment is up to 4x higher than senior unemployment in North Macedonia and Spain in 2024

100%

All analysed countries recovered to or exceeded their pre-COVID employment levels by 2024

85%

Iceland leads Europe with an 85.2% employment rate and just 3.65% unemployment in 2024

1.8pp

Finland has the smallest gender employment gap in Europe — virtually equal participation between men and women
